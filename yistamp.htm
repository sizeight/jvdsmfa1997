Installation

 

 



Electronic installation sculpture already started to appear in the 60s. As discussed in my research for 'An Electronic Laager', Chapter 3, Video Art. The 1968 Museum of Modern Art exhibition, The Machine as Seen at the End of the Mechanical Age , summed up an entire epoch and pointed towards the new electronic era ahead. The curator, Pontus Hulten, meant the exhibition to be a cultural response, to herald what he saw as the transitional movement from the machine-age culture of a manufacturing society based on machines as the 'muscle' of industry, to an electronic information society based on instant communication services.
