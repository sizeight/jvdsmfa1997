4.POVERTY CLOCK

The idea of simulating the growth of virtual cornfields being planted while experiencing the Crossings Project, I started to develop the concept further. Looking for a source of data to 'feed' the growth of the virtual cornplants, I came upon the Eradicating Poverty website [13] of The United Nations Development Programme (UNDP). On that website I found the "Poverty Clock." [Figure 12] The UNDP created the Poverty Clock to illustrate how quickly poverty grows. Each successive digit on the clock indicates another person living on less than $1.00 (U.S.) per day.

Figure 12: The United Nations Development Programme's "Poverty Clock."
(UNDP 1996)

4.1. Calculation

The number of people living in absolute poverty is increasing by nearly 25 million a year. This calculation is based on the World Bank and the United Nations definition of absolute poverty: those people whose incomes are no more than $370 a year, or approximately $1.00 (U.S.) per day. The number of people currently living in absolute poverty is roughly 1.3 billion.

Recent data from the World Bank and the Human Development Report suggest that the aggregate number of poor people worldwide is increasing at roughly the same rate as the annual population growth of the developing world, or about 1.88 per cent. Based on these statistics, the Poverty Clock is ticking off the number of new people living in poverty at approximately 47 per minute.

According to the UNDP the "calculation is based on extensive consultations with population statisticians at the UN Population Trends Unit, and the World Bank Statistics Office. Other sources were a study, 'Rooting Out Poverty,' by Actionaid, and a paper, 'Is Poverty Increasing in the Developing World?' by Martin Ravallion, Shaohua Chen and Gaurav Datt of the World Bank." [14]

4.2. Activated

James Gustave Speth, Administrator of the UNDP, officially activated the clock on January 17 1996, at 18:30 GMT [15]. The idea of the clock formed part of the commemoration of 1996 as the International Year for the Eradication of Poverty. [Figure 13 - 15] "The world has the material and natural resources, the know-how and the people to make a poverty-free world a reality," Mr. Speth said. "One hundred and sixty years ago, the world launched a world crusade against slavery. Today I call upon you to launch a similar crusade for a world without poverty."
