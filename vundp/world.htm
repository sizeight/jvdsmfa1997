WORLD ALLIANCE OF CITIES AGAINST POVERTY

The World Alliance of Cities Against Poverty is an initiative of UNDP's European office in Geneva, in coordination with the UNDP Division of Public Affairs (DPA), New York. It aims to raise public awareness and mobilize energies, during the International Year for the Eradication of Poverty (1996) and beyond, for new initiatives to combat poverty throughout the decade.

UNDP is enroling as many cities as possible in the Alliance through its 137 offices worldwide. It aims at ensuring that all countries are represented. Membership is acquired upon ratification by the city government of the "Appeal of Cities Against Poverty" (over); and the purchase of "shares" sold to defray the costs of global anti-poverty communication campaigns coordinated by the European Office and DPA. Each city is encouraged to buy from one to ten shares at US$500 for industrialized country, and US$100 dollars for developing country.

Aberdeen, Bern, Bonn, Cochabamba, Delémont, Frankfurt, Genoa, Herning, Kolding, Lillehammer, Lisbon, Liège, Madrid, Prez Zeledon, Rome, Rosario, Tromsc and Yerevan are among the first cities to have joined the Alliance.

By raising public awareness about the nature, causes and prevalence of poverty, as well as its devastating impact on human wellbeing, the Alliance hopes to activate a wave of new anti-poverty initiatives by all sectors of society, including individuals, associations, businesses and institutions. Cities are also encouraged to take action locally and internationally to give strength and impact to their commitment.
