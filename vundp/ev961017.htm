COMMEMORATION OF THE INTERNATIONAL DAY FOR THE ERADICATION OF POVERTY
OCTOBER 17-24

In rich countries such as the U.S., more than 100 million people live in poverty and 5 million are homeless. While rich people in the U.S. are getting richer, the number of people living below the poverty line is actually increasing. And, the U.S. is first in AIDS cases, ranks 18th in life expectancy and is first in annual environmental degradation among rich countries.

The figures are even more troubling on a global level. Today, 1.3 billion people or one-third of the world's population lives in poverty. Each year, 25 million more people fall into the depths of this social scourge. More than a third of the world's children is malnourished.

Given the urgency and magnitude of the poverty challenge, the United Nations General Assembly declared 1996 the International Year for the Eradication of Poverty.

October 17, the International day for the Eradication of Poverty, provides a unique opportunity to reflect on the stark reality that is poverty -- lack of sufficient food, safe water, reliable health care adequate shelter education, training and income-earning. The events planned by UNDP on that date and the activities which will continue through October 24 (United Nations Day), serve as a vivid call to action and civic responsibility.

Through these events, UNDP makes a public plea to all citizens to begin to demonstrate active solidarity for the masses of poor people around the world. For, according to the Human Development Report, poverty affects all countries, not merely the poorest of the world's nations. It extends beyond the confines of inner cities just as it surpasses national borders. And the consequences of poverty -- drugs, forced migration, environmental degradation, diseases such as AIDS and terrorism travel without a passport and stop at no frontier.

The attached schedule of events will provide you with details on what is being planned. We look forward to seeing you.
