UNDP PLANS SPECIAL EVENTS IN NEW YORK ON THURSDAY TO MARK 17 OCTOBER, POVERTY ERADICATION DAY

New York, 17 October 1996 -- A large number of special events are being planned today by UNDP offices around the world to mark Poverty Eradication Day, 17 October, as proclaimed by the UN General Assembly. The following events are scheduled in New York:

A Multi-Religious Prayer Service, which will take place at 9:15 am at the Tillman Chapel, Church Center for the United Nations. Scheduled speakers include: Norma Levitt, Honorary President, World Conference on Religion and Peace; Livia Sylva, New York City Commissioner for the United Nations and Normand Lauzon, UNDP Assistant Administrator and Director, UNDP Bureau for Resources and External Affairs. Prayers will be offered by Baha'i, Buddhist, Christian, Hindu, Jewish and Muslim representatives.

At 10:30 AM on Thursday, James Gustave Speth, Administrator of UNDP, will be among the speakers to address representatives of non-governmental organizations in the Dag Hammerskjold Library Auditorium at the UN. The subject of Mr. Speth's address at the NGO BRIEFING, organized by the UN Department of Public Information, is "UNDP: Action Against Poverty."

At 12:00 on Thursday, Mr. Speth will address the press at the daily briefing of the UN Secretary-General Spokesperson in room 226 at the UN Secretariat. Mr. Speth will speak on the global challenge of poverty and on UNDP efforts to help eradicate it.

For more information on the above events please contact UNDP: Cassandra Waldon, 212-906-6499 or Sid Kane, 212-906-5324; fax 212-906-5364.
