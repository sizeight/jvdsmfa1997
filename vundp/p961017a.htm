UNDP EVENTS AROUND THE WORLD MARK 17 OCTOBER AS DAY FOR POVERTY ERADICATION

Historic Day Leads to Wide Range of Global Activities

New York, 17 October 1996 -- United Nations Development Programme offices around the world have declared the week of 17-24 October "Poverty Week". Special events will help publicize the plight of the 1.3 billion people who live on less than US$1 a day, and the need for global and local actions to counter poverty's harsh effects. On 17 October, the International Day for the Eradication of Poverty, UNDP will help sponsor activities in over 100 countries to mark this historic occasion of international solidarity against poverty.

James Gustave Speth, Administrator of UNDP, emphasized in a statement released today that the world has the material and natural resources, the know-how and the people to make a poverty-free world a reality. "We have committed the full force of all our energies to fight poverty, the gravest challenge of our time," Mr. Speth said.

UNDP has targeted poverty eradication as its top priority, and is engaged in a coordinated effort by the UN system to help governments attack its root causes and harsh consequences.

On 17 October, an interfaith service will be held at the UN Church Center in New York, organized by UNDP and the World Conference on Religion and Peace. That same day Mr. Speth will meet with representatives of 150 NGOs to mobilize civil society in the fight against poverty. He will also address press at a noon briefing at the UN.

Mayors of 40 cities in the US and Canada, among them New York, Philadelphia and Washington, DC, have joined a drive launched by UNDP and proclaimed 17 October as Poverty Eradication Day.

UNDP has invited staff of all UN agencies around the world to make a donation to anti-poverty projects and to fast on 18 October.

UNDP and the UNA-USA will announce winners of the Students Against Poverty Contest in the US -- a competition to come up with ideas to help combat poverty in their communities.

In more than 100 countries, tens of thousands of people are participating in sports and cultural events -- walks, runs, football and handball matches, music and art festivals, and even a calypso contest -- to publicize Poverty Eradication Day. In some countries citizens will distribute food and clothing to vulnerable groups and communities in need. People from all walks of life are taking part in the many events, including presidents and mayors, representatives from youth and women's organizations, UN officials and representatives from media and NGOs.

Additionally, to stimulate debate on poverty eradication, UNDP has selected poverty as the theme for the 1997 Human Development Report.

"Unless we address the problems of poverty right now, none of the great goals the international community has set -- peace, stability, human rights for all, preservation of the environment -- are achievable", said Mr. Speth.

The UN General Assembly, in resolution 50/107, established 1996 as the International Year for the Eradication of Poverty, and 1996-2006 as the International Decade for the Eradication of Poverty.
