POVERTY WITNESSES SPEAK OUT AND "POVERTY CLOCK" IS ACTIVATED BY HEAD OF UNDP TO MARK ANTI-POVERTY YEAR

NEW YORK, 17 JANUARY 1996 -- "We must never forget that behind every tick of the clock there is a face, a person, a life," said James Gustave Speth, Administrator of the United Nations Development Programme, as he started a "poverty clock" ticking in the lobby of the United Nations building here today. The event recognized 1996 as the International Year for the Eradication of Poverty. The clock ticks every time another person joins the ranks of the poor, some 47 a minute or more than 67,000 every day.

Three of the people whose lives have been touched by poverty but who are working their way out of poverty travelled to the UN from Zimbabwe, Bolivia and Harlem (New York) to tell their tales of fighting against poverty in their communities:

· Betty Bangira, a Zimbabwean mother of four, found her life changed when her community joined with five others to create an irrigation project in one of the country's desertified provinces;

· Luis Ticona Mamani, an indigenous Aymara of the Bolivian highlands, is now the director of an association of alpaca herdsmen which has tripled incomes of families in the region;

· Lenore Cola, a mother of three and a Harlem resident whose family was homeless for four years, became involved in 1989 in a street library programme for children and their parents.

"This event is a cause for both alarm and optimism," said UNDP Administrator Speth. "Alarm because we are turning on a poverty clock which will remind us that each day another 67,000 people join the ranks of the poor. That's about 25 million people a year, most of whom will be in the developing world. Optimism because the international community now recognizes the urgency of this problem, realizes what must be done and is taking steps to halt, indeed reverse, this grim trend."

UNDP has made poverty eradication the overarching goal of its programmes, working through 135 offices in developing countries, and building its expertise in the area. The agency is supporting developing countries as they build or strengthen national poverty eradication strategies, setting timed targets for progress.

The ceremony was enlivened by the pan flute of Jorge Cabrera, an Ecuadoran musician who participates in the Metropolitan Transit Authority's Music Underground programme.

Cooperating with UNDP in sponsoring the event was International Movement ATD Fourth World, a non-governmental organization dedicated to helping individuals and families free themselves from extreme poverty, and which successfully pressed to have October 17 declared the International Day for Poverty Eradication. The street library programme of Ms. Cola is organized by ATD Fourth World.
