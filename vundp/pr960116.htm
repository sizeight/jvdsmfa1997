POVERTY TALES, "POVERTY CLOCK" WILL MARK INTERNATIONAL YEAR FOR THE ERADICATION OF POVERTY

Nineteen ninety-six, the International Year for the Eradication of Poverty (IYEP), provides a unique opportunity to acknowledge the daily efforts made by women and men worldwide, in developed as well as developing countries, to overcome poverty.

We are pleased to invite you to a special event which will highlight the stories of people and communities who have struggled against poverty, sometimes with outstanding success. The event, in support of the International Year for the Eradication of Poverty, will take place on Wednesday, 17 January 1996, at 1:00 p.m. in the Visitors' Lobby of the UN Building.

The ceremony is organized by the UN Development Programme in collaboration with the International Movement ATD Fourth World. In addition to first-hand testimonials, UNDP's "Poverty Clock", which tallies the number of people born into poverty each minute, will be turned on by UNDP Administrator James Gustave Speth, to run throughout the year.

DATE: Wednesday 17 January 1996

TIME: 1:00 - 1:30 p.m.

VENUE: Visitors' Lobby, UN Building
