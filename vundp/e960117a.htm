WINNING THE FIGHT AGAINST POVERTY

ames Gustave Speth, Administrator of the United Nations Development Programme, has turned on a poverty clock, which shows that every day, 67,000 people join the ranks of the world's poor. The clock is part of the commemoration of 1996 as the International Year for the Eradication of Poverty. "The world has the material and natural resources, the know-how and the people to make a poverty-free world a reality," Mr. Speth said. "One hundred and sixty years ago, the world launched a world crusade against slavery. Today I call upon you to launch a similar crusade--for a world without poverty."

Three who are beating the Poverty Clock

etty Bangira , a Zimbabwean mother of four, says she is escaping poverty thanks to an irrigation project that has enabled her to grow more vegetables. Ms. Bangira speaks in front of the "poverty clock" of the United Nations Development Programme.
