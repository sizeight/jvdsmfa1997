THE VIRTUAL HARVESTER project explores how computer simulation combined with data input from the world wide web can be applied for art projects, specifically towards creating interactive virtual environments.

The United Nations Development Programme created the Poverty Clock to illustrate how quickly poverty grows. Each successive digit on the clock indicates another person living on less than $1 per day.

Making use of the harbour simulator facility in Rotterdam, the viewer will be surrounded by virtual corn fields, each plant representing another person living in poverty. Synchronized to the Poverty Clock's ticking, one new plant sprouts on the virtual corn field; yielding approximately 47 plants each minute.
